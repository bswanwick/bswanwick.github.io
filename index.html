My history with computing

I was born a little late for the early days of Usenet and BBS, but I remember our local ISP had pay per minute websites for certain games and content. Dialup charges to the tune of $1 ‐ $5 / minute! I remember my first computer was a windows 4.1 machine. We played Lemmings and Snake. I found a "Python" icon somewhere on the C: drive and thought it was another game like Snake. The indecipherable UI and text therein mystified me. From there, I was writing my own games on a TI-83. C++ was my first real language, followed by Java, and C#.

Now I'm an engineering leader and product development specialist. I've brought multiple apps and platforms from idea to production for international banks, presitigous universities, airports, airlines, chemists, and the federal government. It's been a wild ride, and I can't wait to see what's next!
